Let's Talk Birds.

Get to know our avian friends.

Birds are feathered, warm-blooded creatures that have wings and can lay eggs.
There are around 10,000 different species of birds around the world!
Scientists believe that birds evolved from theropod dinosaurs.
Pretty cool, right?

Theropods are meat-eating dinosaurs, the Tyrannosaurus Rex is in this family. Birds evolved from smaller theropods.

Below you will find four types of different birds, click their picture or title to learn more information about them!
Check out the fancy flamingos in their natural habitats, or perhaps the pretty penguins capture your curiousity.

Did you know?

When it comes to birds, it is the males that tend to have the more fabulous feather forms, coloration, dances and songs. Female birds choose their mate on the basis of how attracted they are to them!

~ Flamingos ~

Known for their gorgeous pink feathers, s-shaped necks and scrawny legs, these social creatures can be found congregating in shallow waters.
A group of flamingos is called a flamboyance!

~ Penguins ~

These aquatic, flightless birds are recognised for their black and white coats and flippers. Such a colour scheme enables a camoflauge called 'countershading'.
On land, a group of penguins are called a waddle but in water they're called a raft!

~ Robins ~

Let's bring it back to some of the more common birds - the robin! These little guys are typically grey or brown, with a red patch on their chest and stomach area.
A group of robins is called a 'round'.

~ Toucans ~

Renowned for their bright and colourful beaks, these gorgeous birds reside in the tropics of South America.
Toucans live in small groups called 'flocks' that can contain up to 6 members.
