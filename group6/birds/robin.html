General Information

The robin, or Erithacus rubecula, is a small bird that is a member of the thrush family.

Appearance

Their chests are orange and their faces are grey with a brown back and white or grey belly.

Diet

Robins are omnivores, and they particularly enjoy meal worms. Other foods they eat include beetles, worms, fruits and seeds.

Distribution

They are spread throughout Europe and Asia as well North America.

Species

There are 7 subspecies of the American robin, and where they're situated can have a big change in their plumage!

Robin Fun Facts

Robins are the national animals of Great Britain.

In the past, robins were killed for their meat but now there are protection acts.

Robins are fiesty birds that will fight for their territory to the death.

During the Victorian era, ladies wore robins' skin as a bizarre hat accessory.

The average lifespan of a robin is two years, the longest robin lived holds a record of eight years.

More Robin Information You'll Flock To!

Both parents take responsibility for their young when feeding, taking turns to look after their chicks until they are two weeks old and can leave the nest to become their independent selves.

When a male robin finds a mate, he brings the female food to strengthen their bond and she happily tweets back and flaps her wings. The average observer might mistake them for a mother feeding her young!
